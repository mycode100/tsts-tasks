Lions

Lions, the "king of the jungle," are powerful big cats found in Africa and India. Living in social groups called prides, they hunt large herbivores like zebras. Distinguished by their manes and roars, lions face threats from habitat loss and poaching, making conservation vital for their survival.

Tigers

Tigers are majestic, solitary big cats native to Asia, known for their striking orange coats with black stripes. As apex predators, they primarily hunt large ungulates like deer and wild boar. Tigers face severe threats from habitat loss, poaching, and human conflict, making conservation efforts critical for their survival.

Wolves

Wolves are highly social and intelligent carnivores found in North America, Europe, and Asia. Living in packs, they rely on cooperation to hunt large prey like deer and elk. Wolves communicate through howls, body language, and scent marking. They face threats from habitat loss and human conflict, making conservation important for their survival.

Cheetahs

Cheetahs are the fastest land animals, capable of reaching speeds up to 60-70 mph in short bursts. Native to Africa and parts of Iran, they are characterized by their slender bodies, long legs, and distinctive black "tear marks" on their faces. Cheetahs hunt primarily during the day, targeting small to medium-sized ungulates. They face threats from habitat loss, human-wildlife conflict, and low genetic diversity, making conservation efforts crucial for their survival.
